Introduction to UniMoE-Audio

Unified Speech and Music Generation with Dynamic-Capacity Mixture of Experts

UniMoE-Audio

We propose UniMoE-Audio, a unified speech and music generation model based on a novel dynamic-capacity Mixture-of-Experts framework.


By integrating a dynamic-capacity routing strategy for adaptive resource allocation and a hybrid expert design for functional decoupling, our architecture effectively mitigates the inherent task conflict between speech and music generation.


Challenges in Unifying Speech and Music Generation

The unification of speech and music generation faces :

Two Major Obstacles:

  • 1. Task Conflict:
    • Speech generation: focuses on semantic intelligibility and speaker identity.
    • Music generation: emphasizes harmony, rhythm, and complex structures.
      • Conflict arises because improvements in one task may degrade the other.
    • Potential solution: Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architectures show promise, but their optimization for unified audio remains unresolved.
  • 2. Data Imbalance:
    • Speech corpora: abundant, large-scale, high-quality.
      • Music datasets: relatively scarce.
    • Naive joint training often lets speech dominate, harming musical generation quality.

Here are our work's novelty and contributions.


A novel dynamic-capacity MoE for mitigating task conflict.

Two key architectural optimization:

  • A Dynamic-capacity Routing Strategy:   Enabling more flexible expert combinations
    A routing strategy dynamically adjusts the number of experts allocated to each token based on their complexity.
    That replaces the conventional fixed-capacity routing but based on the Top-P sampling.
  • A Hybrid Expert Design:   Establishing clear functional specialization
    It comprises:
    • (1) Conditional dynamic experts for domain-specific knowledge
    • (2) Constantly active shared experts to handle domain-agnostic features
    • (3) Null experts to enable adaptive computation skipping

Together, these advances form a powerful and efficient framework for high-quality, flexible audio generation across multiple modalities.

A tightly coupled, three-stage training curriculum to address data imbalance.

The curriculum unfolds as follows:

  • Independent Specialist Training leverages the original, uncurated datasets to instill domain-specific knowledge into each ”proto-expert” without interference.
  • MoE Fusion and Warmup then integrates these specialists into the UniMoE-Audio architecture. This stage begins by creating a curated, balanced dataset via a rigorous data filtering pipeline.
  • Synergistic Joint Training finally trains the entire, model end-to-end on the full, curated balanced dataset, fostering effective knowledge transfer across domains.

The comparison between the input (left) and output (right) expert distributions shows a clear shift from nearly uniform usage across all experts to more specialized patterns. In the input, experts are invoked almost evenly at each layer, indicating no strong preference. However, in the output, certain experts dominate at specific depths: lower layers favor experts 1 and 2, middle layers rely more on experts 3-5, and higher layers increasingly use experts 8 and 9. This demonstrates that training encourages a division of labor among experts, where different layers specialize in leveraging particular experts rather than distributing usage uniformly.
